Fernando Maymí

Fernando Maymí is the Deputy Director of the Army Cyber Institute (ACI) at West Point. The ACI is a national resource for research, advice and education in the cyber domain, engaging Army, government, academic and industrial cyber communities to build intellectual capital. Prior to joining the ACI, Dr. Maymí was an Assistant Professor in the Department of Electrical Engineering and Computer Science at the United States Military Academy. In this capacity, he taught courses in Cyber Security, Information Technology and Software Systems Development. He also worked as a Senior Research Scientist in the department’s Cyber Research Center, which is a research center devoted to applied research in the areas of Cyber Operations. Dr. Maymí holds two patents with another pending, is a recipient of the U.S. Army Research and Development Achievement Award, and was named a Luminary by the Hispanic Engineer National Achievement Awards Corporation (HENAAC). He is a retired Army who was awarded fifteen medals including two Defense Meritorious Service Medals. Fernando Maymí holds a Doctorate of Philosophy in Computer and Information Science and Engineering from the University of Puerto Rico at Mayaguez, a Master of Science degree in Computer Science from the Naval Postgraduate School in Monterey, California, and a Bachelor of Science degree from the United States Military Academy in West Point, New York. He is a native of San Juan, Puerto Rico.
